Course

The goal of this course is to get you familiar with numerical tools used in scientific modelling. By the time you finish it you should be able to comfortably and independently approach a variety of astrophysical problems. The course is assignment oriented meaning you will receive a new problem each week and be given a week to solve it and write a report. In general, Tuesday session will be used for introducing new problems and giving you the details on how to approach a particular problem. On Thursdays, we'll be reviewing and discussing the work you have completed. Solving problems will in all cases require you to write a short computer program. The choice of the programming language will not be enforced, but it is highly recommended that you use python programming language. In recent years it has become a standard choice among astronomers. You are encouraged to discuss and develop your solutions with your colleagues but final reports must be produced completely individually.

Software/reports

To successfully solve problems you will be required to use different numerical methods. Instead of writing everything from scratch, you are advised to use already written and well-tested methods (random generators, integrators, FFT and plotting routines etc.) that you can find in various libraries. (This is another reason to stick to python since there is a huge number of very useful python modules available.) Your reports must be written electronically. Preferred typesetting package, at least among scientist, is LaTeX but you are not obligated to use it. You must however submit you report in PDF format with a specific file name: %02d_vuusername.pdf. If your name is John Doe and you are submitting a report for the 4th assignment, you should name your PDF file as 04_jdoe.pdf. Also, reports must be submitted before the deadline.

Grading

Each assignment will be graded on the scale from 0 to 10, depending on the quality, innovativeness, and completeness of your work. Assignments submitted late cannot get more than half of available points, so it is very important that you submit them in time. If an illness or another serious reason prevents you from completing the work, you must notify me in advance (before the submission deadline). Final graded will be generated from the (possibly weighted) average of all assignment grades by following university's grading scale.

Academic integrity

Cheating, copying, or any other violations of the Code of ethics will be taken very seriously and will be strictly reported. Just don't do it.
